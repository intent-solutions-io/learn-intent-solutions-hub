No mandatory prerequisites — the credential is awarded on exam performance alone. Recommended (not required): 3+ years in systems architecture or platform engineering, 6+ months hands-on Claude/LLM production experience, end-to-end delivery experience.

More evenly spread than the Foundations exams — Integration, Solution Design, and Evaluation together are only half the exam. Governance and stakeholder communication carry real weight here; this exam tests architects, not just builders.

Who it's for
  • Mid-to-senior technical professionals who design, build, and deliver production-grade AI solutions — solution architects, AI/ML engineers, technical leads, senior software engineers.
  • Own or significantly contribute to the full lifecycle of a Claude-powered system: discovery, design, handoff, monitoring, iteration.
  • Regularly engage with stakeholders — advising clients or internal teams, leading architectural decisions including security, legal, and executive considerations.
  • Not intended for entry-level developers or casual users — see Developer — Foundations or Associate — Foundations if you're earlier in that path.
How to study

A diagnostic loop

  1. Take a full timed practice test cold.
  2. Score it by domain — the domain split is the signal, the total is vanity.
  3. For every miss, identify whether it was a technical gap or a tradeoff judgment call.
  4. Patch only the weakest domain.
  5. Repeat until two consecutive tests clear 800 with no domain below ~70%.
Discipline When two answers look right, pick the one that removes risk or unnecessary capability rather than the one that monitors or compensates for it — least privilege over logging, architecture over patching.
