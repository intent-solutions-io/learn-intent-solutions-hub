01

The opportunity

Anthropic launched its first official certifications in 2026: four exams across Associate, Developer, and Architect tracks. There is now a credential worth preparing for, and no established place to prepare for it as a group. Intent Solutions runs that place. The hub already prepares members for all four exams and captures what confuses them. What it does not yet have is the community layer that turns a study tool into a program people stay in.

The ask, in one line

Greenlight the community platform buildout — real accounts, member profiles, and a posting feed — so the hub becomes a cohort people belong to, not a site they visit once before an exam.

What we will not claim

The program prepares people; it does not guarantee certification or employment. Anthropic administers and scores the exams. Every "official" fact is sourced and cited; our own guidance is labeled as ours. No invented members, metrics, or testimonials — ever. These are hard rules, and they are already enforced across the live hub.

05

The community platform

Three things stand between the hub today and a real community: identity, posting, and moderation. The elegant path keeps it simple and safe for a known cohort.

Sign in

Passwordless magic links, gated to a cohort roster. No passwords, no open signup, no bot surface. Reading stays public; only posting needs an account.

Profiles

A member's profile is their roster entry, filled in on first sign-in. No separate account-creation flow to build or maintain.

Posting & feed

Attributed posts, categories, and light moderation. Every author is a verified member, which is what keeps the feed honest.

Why magic links, not passwords or Google login

For a known, finite cohort, a magic link collapses sign-up, log-in, and identity-check into one email. The roster is the gate, so there is nothing to abuse and no fake accounts. Passwords would mean owning reset flows and a credential we are liable for; social login would still need the same roster on top. Read is public, write needs a verified member, membership is a list we control.

Built on the stack we already run
Cloudflare Workers Hono routing D1 + Drizzle better-auth magic links Resend email

No new platform to learn and no new vendor relationship of consequence. The one genuinely new dependency is an email sender for the magic links, and email is the single thing that can fail quietly, so it is the piece we harden first.

08

Risks & guardrails

Email deliverability. Magic links live or die on email arriving. We harden the sender first and give members a clear path when a link does not land.
Privacy of a live cohort. Real names and posts mean real responsibility. Access controls and data handling stay conservative; we do not expose member data or compile it across sources.
Moderation load. A feed needs tending. Phase 3 builds the controls; until then, posting stays small and close to us.
Partner-sensitive questions. These route to you in one batch, not many members contacting Anthropic independently. Already the rule on the live hub.
No fabricated community. Empty states stay honest until real members fill them. We never seed fake posts to look busier than we are.
09

Recommendation

Start Phase 1 — identity and the roster — as soon as you confirm who is in the cohort. It is the smallest, safest unlock, it changes nothing members can see until we are ready, and every later phase depends on it. Phases 2 and 3 follow only once Phase 1 is proven.

Everything else in this proposal is already live. The community platform is the difference between a hub people use before an exam and a program they stay in after it. The one thing blocking the first move is your roster.
